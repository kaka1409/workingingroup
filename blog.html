Khánh - The Lust for Power: Finding Peace Within

For a time, desire consumed my life. I wasn’t just chasing physical pleasure; I was driven by an insatiable hunger that I believed would make me feel whole. I was constantly searching for relationships and encounters, believing that they would fill the void inside me. But no matter how deeply I indulged, the satisfaction was always fleeting, leaving me empty and regretful.

I remember going through many relationships, younger to older, each hoping that this would be the one that would make me feel whole. But the more I pursued these desires, the more empty I felt. It was a cycle of temporary pleasure followed by lasting emptiness, and I couldn’t seem to escape it.

Not long after, I joined a group that held a different perspective. Initially, I had difficulty connecting with them because of my conflicting views. Despite this, they chose me as their leader, a role I resisted because I was focused on satisfying my own desires. We had disagreements, and at one point I even suggested disbanding the group. Surprisingly, they did not respond with anger. Instead, they guided me through methods to deal with my lustful thoughts. Although it was difficult to change long-standing habits, I eventually adopted their approach and found it to be truly transformative.

Now, desire no longer rules my life; I am my own master. I have mastered the art of knowing what is most important in life—authentic relationships and living up to my own moral expectations. This change has brought me more joy than ever before.

Nguyên : Lifting the Curse of Sloth

For years, I allowed laziness to dictate my life, indulging in endless procrastination and avoiding the responsibilities that loomed over me. I preferred comfort and ease over the challenges that could have pushed me to grow. The more I gave in to this vice, the harder it became to break free. Initially, the lack of effort felt like a small indulgence, a way to escape the pressures of life. But as time passed, this laziness began to consume me, leading to missed opportunities, unfulfilled dreams, and a growing sense of dissatisfaction with my life. Despite the nagging guilt and the small voice in my head urging me to change, I found it easier to retreat further into my lethargy. I tried my best to ignore the consequences of my inaction, convincing myself that tomorrow would be different. But with each passing day, I slipped further into a cycle of avoidance and regret.

One evening, as I sat alone in my cluttered room, surrounded by the remnants of tasks left undone, I was struck by a sudden realization: "Everything in life depends on how that life accepts its limits." I had been so focused on avoiding discomfort that I failed to recognize the importance of pushing beyond my own self-imposed boundaries. In that moment, I understood that laziness had become my prison, and the key to escaping it lay in accepting the responsibility for my own life. I began to take small steps, setting manageable goals and forcing myself to follow through, even when it felt uncomfortable. I learned to accept that growth required effort and that avoiding challenges only led to stagnation. As I slowly broke free from the grip of laziness, I discovered a newfound sense of purpose and fulfillment. Life became richer and more rewarding as I embraced the value of hard work and perseverance. In the end, learning to overcome laziness was not just about breaking a bad habit; it was about reclaiming my life and realizing that true satisfaction comes from embracing the effort it takes to achieve our goals.

Thành : Arrogant - the shattered Ego

When I was in secondary school, I developed an unsettling belief that I was the center of the entire world. With the ability to learn things quickly and excel in most subjects without much effort, I became convinced that I was a genius, perhaps even someone destined for greatness. This belief fed my arrogance, and I started to act like a jerk, dismissing others as inferior and irrelevant. I would correct teachers in class, scoff at my classmates' mistakes, and brush off anyone who dared challenge me. I truly believed that I was special, that the world revolved around me, and that everyone else was just too slow to keep up.

This delusion of grandeur persisted until I met someone who shattered my inflated self-image. He was a new student, quiet and unassuming, but when he spoke or worked, his brilliance was undeniable. He solved problems faster than I could, asked questions that I hadn’t even considered, and effortlessly grasped concepts that I had struggled with. Watching him, I felt my arrogance begin to crumble. For the first time, I realized that I wasn’t the smartest person in the room, let alone the world. My confidence, once so unshakable, turned into doubt.

Confronted with someone who was clearly better than me, I had no choice but to accept the harsh truth: I was not the center of the world. It was a humbling experience, one that forced me to reevaluate not only my abilities but also my attitude. I began to understand that true genius wasn’t just about being smart or quick to learn; it was about humility, curiosity, and the willingness to keep growing. The realization was a bitter pill to swallow, but it marked the beginning of a much-needed change in me. I learned that the world is far bigger than my own ego, and that there are always people out there who can teach me something new. In the end, meeting someone better than me was the best thing that could have happened; it pulled me out of my arrogance and set me on a path toward genuine self-improvement.

Thắng - Shedding the Burden of Envy

I used to be the kind of person who let envy dictate how I lived my life. Whenever I saw other people achieving great things in their life, I couldn't help but feel envious. This included not just milestones like birthdays and graduations, but also instances when I got to play video games with their beloved characters. I didn't realise it at the time, but I felt that their success was primarily due to luck, as if it were somehow related to me. I chose to be envious because I focused on what I lacked rather than proud of what I had achieved. This not only caused me to lose several relationships over time, but it also changed who I am.

But when I joined this special group of people, things started to shift. Even though I was in the company of kind and supportive individuals, I realised that I had been making mistakes before and was still making them now. Since every person making a life transition will be different from everyone else while yet sharing some similarities, there is no one best way to go. Life is full of problems and difficulties, and everyone deals with them in their own unique manner. You can't go wrong by taking any other course of action. After a long period of time had gone, I started to appreciate parts of myself, like other people's achievements and happiness, and the emotions of jealousy started to go away.

From the moment I first began to see the world, my viewpoint has changed considerably. Jealousy no longer drives me because I've realised that being successful is all about wanting to grow, both personally and in terms of helping other people do the same. I will be forever grateful to this community for helping me become a more complete person.
